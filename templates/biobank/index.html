BioRepository Services

Advancing Neurological Research Through High-Quality Biospecimens

{# We support groundbreaking neurological research by providing high-quality, #} {#well-characterized biospecimens to scientists and clinicians worldwide. Our state-of-the-art facility specializes in the #} {#collection, processing, and long-term storage of blood and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) from both healthy donors and #} {#individuals with neurological conditions.#} The Weill NeuroBioRepository is a Neurology Department core facility established to support and accelerate research into the mechanisms of complex neurological disorders. Our primary function is the systematic collection, processing, and long-term archival of high-quality human biospecimens obtained through our collaborating neurology centers. The repository is strategically focused on major neurodegenerative and neuroinflammatory conditions, including Multiple Sclerosis (MS), Parkinson's Disease, Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), and a spectrum of memory and aging disorders. Our holdings consist of

longitudinally collected cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) and a comprehensive array of blood-derived products, including plasma, serum, peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs), and extracted DNA, providing a robust resource for a wide range of molecular and cellular analyses. The ultimate objective of the biobank is to serve as a foundational platform that enables high-impact neurological research. The curated collections and associated data are structured to support a diverse portfolio of investigations, including longitudinal studies tracking disease progression, the discovery and validation of diagnostic or prognostic biomarkers, and large-

scale genotype-phenotype association studies. By providing centralized, streamlined access to meticulously characterized biospecimens, the biorepository lowers the logistical barriers for investigators to launch ambitious projects,strengthens institutional grant applications, and promotes a campus-wide culture of collaborative, data-driven science. Our commitment is to maintain and grow this resource to the highest scientific and ethical standards, ensuring it remains a powerful engine for discovery.
